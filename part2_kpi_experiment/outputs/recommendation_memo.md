## TASK:1 UNDERSTAND THE BUSINESS PROBLEM

1. What decision needs to be made

The decision is whether the treatment (onboarding and activation campaign) should go live to all users or if the control (the current onboarding experience) should stay in place.

2. Who the decision impacts

New users undergoing the onboarding process product team managing the user experience marketing team concentrating on user acquisition and activation support team dealing with user issues leadership team concerned about growth and revenue

3. What metric should improve

Conversion rate of paid users (Number of users who converted to paid users / Total number of users)

The indicator is needed to be improved as it will measure the success of the campaign in earning income.

4. What risks must be monitored

refund rate increased
support tickets increased
engagement decreased
Poor performance for certain segments of users (by region, by device type, by traffic source, by plan type)

5. What evidence is required before making a recommendation

Control vs. Treatment group performance comparison
Results from statistical hypothesis testing (p-value and significance level)
North Star metric assessment (Paid Conversion Rate)
Guardrail metrics evaluation
Segment-level assessment
Confirmation of significance of the improvement, as well as its statistical significance and lack of business risk



## TASK : 2 DEFINE THE NORTH STAR MATRICS

North Star Metric: Paid Conversion Rate

Paid Conversion Rate serves as the North Star Metric of this experiment as it provides information about the percentage of users that turn into paying subscribers as a result of going through the onboarding experience. As the objective of the onboarding and activation campaign is to generate more paying customers, this is the most appropriate metric to gauge the effectiveness of the experiment.



## TASK 8 : EVALUATE GUARDRAIL METICS

1. Refund Rate

refund rate was measured to see if an increase in conversions would lead to poor customer quality. There was no substantial increase in refund rate, which means low risk.

2. Support Tickets Rate

support tickets rate was checked to find out if there were some usability or onboarding problems. The Treatment group showed no signs of a high number of support tickets.

3. Days to Convert

average days to convert were measured to make sure the new onboarding procedure doesn’t slow down the conversion process. The Treatment group converted customers just as fast or even faster than the Control group.

4. Engagement Score

average engagement score was checked to prove that the users stay active after the onboarding process. Engagement scores stayed the same or grew in the Treatment group.

5. Revenue quality

revenue per user conversion was evaluated to ensure that any increase in conversions also translated to a good amount of revenue. The Treatment group had acceptable quality of revenue.

6. Segment-level decline

performance was evaluated within Region, Device Type, Traffic Source, and Plan Type segments. There was no segment wise decline that could raise concerns for the wider implementation of the feature.

7. Risk Assessment

from the evaluated guardrail metrics, there were no major risks identified. The Treatment group had enhanced Paid Conversion Rate with acceptable rates of refunds, support tickets, engagement, quality of revenue, and segment performance.
